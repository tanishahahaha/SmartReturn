What is GST in India?

GST Definition

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect tax implemented in India on July 1, 2017, replacing multiple cascading taxes levied by the central and state governments. It's a comprehensive, multi-stage, destination-based tax that is applied on every value addition.

Key Features of GST

GST has been designed as a unified tax system that:

  • Eliminates the cascading effect of taxes (tax on tax)
  • Creates a common national market
  • Simplifies the tax structure
  • Reduces hidden costs of doing business
  • Enhances transparency in taxation

GST Structure in India

India has adopted a dual GST model where the tax is levied by both the Central and State governments:

  • CGST (Central GST): Collected by the Central Government
  • SGST (State GST): Collected by the State Government
  • IGST (Integrated GST): Collected on inter-state supply of goods and services
  • UTGST (Union Territory GST): Collected in Union Territories without legislature

Benefits of GST

  • Reduction in overall tax burden on goods
  • Mitigation of cascading effects
  • Development of a common national market
  • Enhanced ease of doing business
  • Improved tax compliance with a robust IT infrastructure

Special Rates

Besides the standard slabs, there are special rates for specific items:

  • 3% on gold, silver, and semi-precious stones
  • 0.25% on rough precious and semi-precious stones
  • 28% + Cess (ranging from 1% to 25%) on luxury and sin goods like tobacco products, luxury cars, and aerated drinks

Components of GST

Central GST (CGST)

CGST is the tax collected by the Central Government on intra-state sales (i.e., sales within the same state). The revenue from CGST goes to the Central Government.

Example: If a product has a GST rate of 18% and is sold within Maharashtra, 9% would be collected as CGST.

State GST (SGST)

SGST is collected by the State Government on intra-state sales. The revenue from SGST goes to the respective State Government.

Example: Using the same example above, the remaining 9% would be collected as SGST for the Maharashtra government.

Integrated GST (IGST)

IGST is collected on inter-state sales of goods and services, as well as on imports. The IGST is collected by the Central Government, which then distributes it between the Center and the destination State.

Example: If a product with an 18% GST rate is sold from Maharashtra to Gujarat, the entire 18% would be collected as IGST.

Union Territory GST (UTGST)

UTGST is collected on intra-UT supply of goods and services in Union Territories without legislature (Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Lakshadweep, Dadra and Nagar Haveli, Daman and Diu, and Chandigarh).

Example: For a product with an 18% GST rate sold within Chandigarh, 9% would be CGST and 9% would be UTGST.

GST Compensation Cess

This is an additional levy on certain luxury and sin goods, designed to compensate states for revenue losses due to GST implementation. The cess is collected by the Central Government and distributed to affected states.

Example: Luxury cars are taxed at 28% GST plus a compensation cess that can range from 1% to 22%.

Non-GST Goods

Certain goods and services remain outside the GST framework and continue to be taxed under the previous tax regime. Here are the major categories:

Petroleum Products

Five petroleum products are currently outside the GST regime:

  • Crude oil
  • Natural gas
  • Petrol
  • Diesel
  • Aviation turbine fuel (ATF)

These products continue to attract Central Excise Duty (for the Center) and Sales Tax/VAT (for the States).

Alcoholic Beverages

Alcoholic beverages for human consumption remain outside the GST framework. States continue to levy state excise duty and sales tax/VAT on alcoholic beverages.

Electricity

Electricity is exempt from GST. Instead, electricity duty is levied by the states.

Real Estate

While construction services fall under GST, the sale of land and completed buildings is outside the GST regime. Stamp duty and registration fees continue to apply on real estate transactions.

Tobacco Products

Tobacco products are subject to GST, but states are also allowed to levy taxes on tobacco products in addition to GST.

Penalties for Late Filing

Late filing of GST returns attracts penalties:

  • A late fee of ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST) for NIL returns
  • A late fee of ₹100 per day (₹50 CGST + ₹50 SGST) for non-NIL returns
  • Maximum late fee capped at ₹5,000

Additionally, interest at 18% per annum is applicable on tax liability not paid by the due date.
